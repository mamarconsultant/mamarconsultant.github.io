Mohamed Amar, independent property consultant, Dubai.

Contact me now

Every morning, Dubai's developers send out their availability: price sheets, launch decks, "final units" lists. Most people never see this flow, and honestly, most of it deserves to be ignored. Undersized "2-bedrooms", balconies bigger than the bedroom, prices that only work in the brochure.

Every deal that reaches me gets my personal evaluation, the same way I'd judge it if I were spending my own money: is the size fair for the unit type, how much of the floor plan do you actually live in, does the outdoor space make sense, does the layout make logical sense. The handful that pass my bar end up on the front page. The rest quietly disappear.

And I do spend my own money. I own and manage a multi-million dirham portfolio of my own units across Dubai, so I live the landlord side of this every month: maintenance on a schedule instead of after a breakdown, renewals timed to the RERA window, tenants who stay because things get fixed fast. When I talk about the cost of a missed AC service or an empty month on the management page, that's not theory. It's my own money teaching me the lesson first.
