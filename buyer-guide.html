What buying in Dubai actually costs

The sticker price is not the full price. Here's every dirham you should budget for, before, during and after the purchase, so nothing surprises you at the trustee office.

The good news first: Dubai has no annual property tax and no capital gains tax on your sale profit. Most of what you pay is one-time, upfront, and predictable.

1. One-time costs when you buy

Rule of thumb: budget roughly 7–8% on top of the purchase price for a cash purchase, or 9–10% with a mortgage.

CostTypical amountNotes
DLD transfer fee4% of purchase priceDubai Land Department fee, due at transfer. On off-plan launches, developers sometimes run "DLD waiver" promotions. Always check who pays.
DLD admin fee~AED 580Fixed fee for apartments and villas.
Trustee office fee~AED 4,200 (incl. VAT)Roughly half that for properties under AED 500k. This is where the transfer actually happens.
Agency fee2% + VATStandard on resale (secondary) purchases. Buying off-plan directly from a developer: usually zero, because the developer pays the commission.
Developer NOCAED 500–5,000Resale only: the developer certifies no outstanding service charges before transfer.
Mortgage arrangementup to 1% of loan + VATBank fee, sometimes negotiable or capped in promotions.
Mortgage registration0.25% of loan + AED 290Paid to DLD when the bank registers its interest.
Valuation feeAED 2,500–3,500 + VATBank-ordered valuation, mortgage purchases only.
Conveyancing (optional)AED 5,000–10,000Worth it for first-time buyers or complex transactions; not mandatory.
Off-plan buyers: your payment plan (e.g. 60/40 or 80/20) spreads the purchase price, but the 4% DLD fee and registration (Oqood) are typically due early. Confirm the exact schedule in the SPA before signing.

2. Ongoing costs while you own

CostTypical amountNotes
Service chargesAED 10–25 per sqft per yearThe big one. Varies hugely by community and tower; premium buildings with big amenity decks run higher. Always ask for the current rate per sqft before you buy: on a 1,000 sqft apartment, AED 15/sqft is AED 15,000 every year.
DEWA housing fee5% of annual rental valueCharged monthly through your DEWA (utility) bill, based on the RERA rental index for your unit. It applies whether you live in it or rent it out.
Utilities & coolingvariesDEWA for electricity/water. Many towers use district cooling (chiller) with its own provider. Ask whether chiller is "free" (included in service charge) or billed separately with capacity charges.
Home insurancefrom ~AED 1,000 / yearOptional but cheap. Building structure is covered via service charges; this covers your contents and liability.
Maintenance reserve~1% of value / yearA sensible budget for AC servicing, appliances and wear, especially for units past their defect-liability period.

3. Costs when you sell

CostTypical amountNotes
Agency fee2% + VATStandard listing commission on the secondary market.
Early mortgage settlement1% of outstanding (capped ~AED 10,000)If you still have a mortgage; plus small release/blocking fees at the bank and DLD.
Developer NOCAED 500–5,000Same certificate as on the way in. The buyer usually attends, you usually pay.
Capital gains taxAED 0Dubai doesn't tax your profit on sale.

Worked example: AED 1.5M apartment, cash purchase

ItemAmount
DLD transfer fee (4%)AED 60,000
DLD admin + trustee fees~AED 4,800
Agency fee (resale, 2% + VAT)AED 31,500
Total on top of price~AED 96,300 (≈6.4%)

Buying the same value off-plan from a developer would typically drop the agency fee to zero. That's one reason developer launches are so popular with first-time buyers.

Figures are typical market rates as of mid-2026 and change over time. Treat them as budgeting guidance, and verify current DLD and bank fees before committing.
