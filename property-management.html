Why timing is everything

Property problems in Dubai share one habit: they get more expensive the longer they wait. A few examples I see over and over:

What gets delayedWhat it turns into
Re-listing after a tenant leavesPure vacancy loss. On a unit renting at AED 90,000, every idle month is AED 7,500 you never get back. Two slow months pay for years of management.
AC servicingIn Dubai heat, a neglected AC doesn't limp along, it fails in August. An emergency compressor replacement costs many times a routine service, and your tenant is on the phone every hour until it's fixed.
A small water leakA stained ceiling, warped cabinets, and if it reaches the apartment below, a neighbour's repair bill with your name on it. Leaks never get cheaper by waiting.
The renewal notice windowRent increases need 90 days' written notice before renewal. Miss the window and you're locked at the old rent for another full year, even if the market moved 15% around you.
Tenant vettingA bounced cheque and a rental dispute case can eat months of rent and a year of patience. Vetting is boring precisely because it works.
Service charge paymentsFall behind and the developer can refuse the NOC when you eventually sell, stalling your transfer at the worst possible moment.
The overseas-owner trap: problems in an unwatched apartment don't announce themselves. They compound quietly until a tenant leaves, a ceiling stains, or a renewal lapses. If you can't visit your unit regularly, someone you trust should.
